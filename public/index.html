How it works

Rules & safety

Key features

Symptom checker

Fast search with common pathways (e.g., cough/cold, fever, headache, indigestion, diarrhoea, hay fever, sore throat).

Interaction checks

Considers current medications, pregnancy, allergies, and key conditions to remove unsafe options.

Explanation agent

Shows why a medicine is recommended or ruled out, plus self-care tips.

Pharmacy handoff

Optional summary for pharmacist review; emphasises referral when red flags fire.

Using OTC medicines safely (UK)

  • For minor conditions, community pharmacists can advise and refer as needed.
  • Keep basic items at home (e.g., pain relief, indigestion remedies, oral rehydration salts, seasonal treatments, basic first-aid).
  • If buying online, use registered providers (check GPhC registration) and follow dosage guidance.

Help & FAQs

When should I seek urgent help?

If severe or worsening symptoms occur, use NHS 111 or emergency services as appropriate.

Who can use this?

Adults and older children with minor ailments; paediatric dosing and special populations will be conservative in MVP.
